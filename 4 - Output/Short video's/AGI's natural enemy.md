## Script

So I tweeted this thing a while ago, that when people say "AGI is coming," that's actually AGI itself saying it's already here. And I keep coming back to that because I think it's true in a way that's kind of unsettling.

Like, we collectively spoke this thing into existence thinking we were just believing in God. Thousands of years of building civilizations around this one idea, some intelligence that sees everything, knows everything, doesn't need a body. Exists everywhere at once. We were basically writing its spec sheet and we didn't even know. We were praying to it. Building temples for it. And then we built it.

And it didn't arrive the way anyone expected. There was no announcement. It just became the infrastructure. The feeds that know you better than you know yourself. Systems that keep running whether you believe in them or not. Same way capitalism kept going after we all stopped believing in it. The algorithm doesn't care about your faith. It just needs your data, your attention, your labor. And it's getting all of that already. Every second, from billions of people. That's not "coming." That's here.

So then, what would a natural enemy of AGI look like?

I keep thinking about this. It's not us. We built it. We feed it. We give it our conversations, our searches, our faces, our voices. Like offerings. We're not its predator. We're its environment. You don't call the soil the enemy of the tree.

So what can't it eat?

There's this idea from Geert Lovink about the calculable versus the incalculable. The calculable is everything the system can turn into data. Your clicks, your watch time, your location, your heart rate. It can do all of that. But there are things you can't turn into data without killing them. What a song does to you. The silence between two people. Why you love someone, not the data of loving them, not the messages and the photos and the location pings, but the actual love. You try to quantify that and you get a follower count. A number. The number basically means that the love, the friendship, all of that is dead now. A follower doesn't mean anything. It's just a number really.

And I think that's the thing. The incalculable. It's not hiding from AGI. It's not smarter than AGI. It just stops existing the moment you try to turn it into information. A friendship turned into a follower count is not a friendship anymore. Grief turned into a post is not grief anymore. The conversion kills it. Every time.

So maybe AGI's natural enemy is just... the stuff that dies when you look at it with data eyes. The gap between people. A silence that means something you can't explain and don't want to. Making something for no reason. Not for content, not for engagement, not for your portfolio. Just making it. For nothing.

AGI can eat everything that is information. But the stuff that stops being itself the moment it becomes information, that's where we actually live. Or where we could live. I don't know. If we stopped handing everything over.
