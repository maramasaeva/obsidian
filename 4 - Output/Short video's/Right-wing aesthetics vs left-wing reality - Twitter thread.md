## @AbyssalTsundere's reply

You must understand that the agenda of the left is fundamentally, as egalitarian idealism, rooted in unreality, as it disregards and disrespects the implicit nature of men as *different*. As a consequence, their art fails to serve the actual intention of art, as the beautiful, as it fails to act in reflection of man's self-affirming nature as an organism. Their art is a deconstructive ugliness alongside an impetus of ressentiment.

The right, at its core (which is an antagonism against the reactionary basis of the left), is the affirmation of nature, excellence, and beauty. It affirms man's primordial being as, in the Heideggerian sense, being is a questioning of itself, where it becomes its own issue — a future-oriented potentializing of something that could be better. Where art is beautiful, it speaks truly to man, as a creature who confronts and embraces the cruelty of nature, and seeks for something better alongside his own, self-differentiating Will to Power.
