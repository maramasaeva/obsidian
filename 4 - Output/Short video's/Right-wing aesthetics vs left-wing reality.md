## What I said

Left-wing arguments are based on reality. Social justice, healthcare, equality, fair treatment of actual people. They want something real to happen, something that hasn't been achieved yet or hasn't been achieved well enough.

Right-wing arguments are based on aesthetics. Look at modern architecture. Look at our culture being endangered. Look at what we're losing. A lot of it can be reduced to a worry about some kind of outward look.

But then there's this contradiction when you look at the art. Right-wing art is hyper-realistic. Romantic, detailed, naturalistic, real human portrayal. Left-wing art is abstract, experimental, conceptual, open to interpretation. The reality people make abstract art. The aesthetics people make realistic art.

And the right has gotten very good at using that. Especially now with the attention economy, with attention spans getting shorter. Remilia, Milady as examples. It's very easy to attract young people with short attention spans by just serving them a very aesthetically pleasing image. Even me, even though I can think critically about it, I find it easy to get pulled into these spaces just by liking the aesthetics, even when I don't agree with the arguments behind it.

They know they can only say something about aesthetics and not about reality. So what's the best way to lure you in? By showing you beautiful art. Because you won't read the whole post. You won't listen to an entire video essay. You'll just scroll past. The only way they can make you stop and click is by making sure that what you see in that split second is beautiful. The right is very good at making beautiful things and has gotten so good at capturing attention.

And then the looksmaxing thing ties into this. The capitalist visual-first culture online has put everyone under the gaze now. Even men, who used to be in power, have succumbed to the same thing women had to deal with for years: being obsessed with how you look, selling yourself all the time. You're under the male or the capitalist gaze almost constantly. Aesthetics is becoming more and more important and reality is fading as we move deeper into the digital age. The digital is reality, but it's a different kind.
