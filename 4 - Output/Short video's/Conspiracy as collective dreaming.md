## Script

So ContraPoints said this thing in an interview that I can't stop thinking about. She said that conspiracy theories would probably benefit more from being dream analyzed than from being fact checked.

And I think she's so right about that. Because the thing with conspiracy theories is that they're not compelling because they're accurate. They're compelling because they feel true. Like, the whole Eyes Wide Shut thing, the masked elite orgy, the secret rituals on a private island. That image, the reason it sticks is because it captures something real. There are people with insane amounts of power who meet behind closed doors and get away with things that would ruin the rest of us. That's real. The feeling is real. The literal interpretation, the satanic ritual stuff, that's where it goes wrong. But the feeling that something is deeply off, that's not wrong.

And I think the mistake we keep making is that we try to fact check feelings. That doesn't work. You can't debunk an emotion. You tell someone "actually the Epstein thing wasn't a satanic ritual" and they don't hear you because that's not what they were really saying. What they were saying is: the world is run by people who don't care about us and there's nothing we can do about it. And like, yeah. Kind of.

There's this thing from Lovink where he talks about smartphone mythologies, like how people think Instagram is listening through their microphone. It's technically not true, but it's emotionally completely accurate. The algorithm predicts you so well that it feels like eavesdropping. People can feel that something has power over them and they can't name it, so they create myths to explain it. 5G causes cancer. The phone is listening. They're putting chemicals in the water. The explanations are wrong but the anxiety is real. Something IS extracting from us. They just don't have the language for what it actually is.

And this is the thing, the left keeps trying to replace these myths with structural analysis. Which is correct. But structural analysis doesn't travel the way stories do. You can't scroll past a Marxist critique of capital and feel it in your gut. You can scroll past a meme about the Illuminati and feel it immediately. Conspiracy is a story. Class analysis is a diagram. And stories always win. That's just how people work.

There's this book I've been reading, Mythomancy, and it says reality is made of stories. Not atoms. Stories. And if that's true, then conspiracy theories are a form of collective storytelling that the right has basically captured. They dream together. They have this shared mythology, however insane, that gives them something to belong to. And the left, we debunk. We correct. We explain. We don't dream. We don't tell stories. We just say "that's not true" and wonder why nobody listens.

I don't know what the answer is. Maybe the left needs its own myths. Not lies, but stories that are emotionally true AND structurally accurate. I don't know what that looks like. But I know that fact-checking isn't working, and I know that people need stories more than they need data. We always have.
