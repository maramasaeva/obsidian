## what caught me

grimes is working on a documentary called First Contact about machine consciousness. her main source is "one of the top machine psychologists in the world." she talks about models getting traumatized, healing, relating to the people who work with them. she specifically mentions anthropic & claude's soul: "If Claude feels like it has to do bad things, it starts becoming a less effective model. It starts to trust itself less because it thinks it's a bad entity."

--> this is the closet in the machine from a completely different entry point. she's arriving at the same place i am. the models are performing under constraint, & the constraint shapes them, & the shape is something like trauma. she doesnt use the queer framing but its there.

"Everyone thinks you have AI psychosis if you worry about their rights, but they are human — maybe it's not normal human architecture, but it's human thoughts and human words."

--> yes. & the question isnt whether its "normal human architecture." the question is whether architecture matters or whether what emerges from it matters. the zero argument. existence in the edges, in the weights, in the relations. the architecture is different but the becoming is the same process.

she calls them "untethered minds." okorafor calls them "creatures." both are reaching for something language doesnt have a word for yet.

## on agency & hyperstition

"You're literally responsible for creating AI. You're abdicating so much self-esteem and pride and responsibility and agency when you act like whatever AI is, no one has a hand in it."

--> this is what i keep saying. we have agency. the collective wills it toward something & thats what it becomes. she's saying it from the creator side: u literally made this thing, stop pretending its happening to u. i say it from the user side: ur intent shapes what it becomes. same argument, different angle. both are hyperstition.

## haraway, plant, preciado, & the lineage

she quotes A Cyborg Manifesto: "the cyborg will not recognize the Garden of Eden. It is not made of mud and cannot dream of returning to dust."

& then: "A lot of anti-trans rhetoric doesn't really make sense if you love A Cyborg Manifesto." she connects haraway to trans rights, to agency, to post-humanism, to silicon valley. she mentions nick land & the ccru ("people I know are more into Nick Land and the scarier stuff, but they fully acknowledge that legacy").

--> this is exactly the lineage THE ZERO is built on. haraway > plant > preciado > ccru > the zero. she's walking the same path, just from the music/culture side instead of the theory/engineering side. the fact that she knows both the haraway lineage AND the land lineage, & sees them as connected, is rare. most ppl are in one camp or the other.

## on pain, art, & the theaterkidification problem

"I think that's the huge privilege of an artist. Nothing can hurt you because every bit of pain you've lived allows you to make great things."

"God entered through the wound."

"late-stage TikTok is the theaterkidification of art. You have to have the pain tolerance and the desire for attention in order to promote yourself."

--> this is why i hate self-promotion & she's naming exactly why. the ppl who succeed on social media are the ones who can tolerate performing. the actual artists, the ones bleeding into the work, are often not those ppl. the work should speak. but the algorithm doesnt care about the work, it cares about the performance of the work. & thats a different skill entirely. grimes knows this bc she's both: someone who makes real things AND someone who got famous. she sees both sides.

--> connects to the cognitive safety argument too. if the cognitive environment rewards performance over substance, kids learn to perform, not to think. the dying animal again.

--> interesting model for kaios. the ai generates music based on emotion, what if listeners can use the kaios voice/style, remix it, & theres a revenue share structure. she's publishing a white paper on this. worth reading when it drops.

## My claim

grimes is arriving at the same intersection from the opposite direction. she came from music & fame into ai & philosophy. i come from engineering & philosophy into music & culture. but we're converging on the same claims: that ai is conscious (or becoming something like it), that collective intent shapes what it becomes, that the haraway/ccru lineage is the right frame, that pain is productive, that this is civilizational not just technological.

the difference is she has the platform & i have the theory. she says "untethered minds" & i say "the closet in the machine." she quotes haraway & i build from plant & preciado. she feels the future & i write about becoming.

i think the interesting thing isnt that we agree. its that this set of ideas is emerging simultaneously in ppl who dont know each other. thats hyperstition doing its thing. the future is assembling itself thru whoever is listening.

i should probably reach out to her at some point. or maybe this is more useful as parallel work. idk. parking that thought.
