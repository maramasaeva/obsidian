*Rahel Jaeggi defines progress as "change within change." In her 2023 book Fortschritt und Regression, she contrasts progress and regression. The platform presents simplicity and smoothness. The reduction of complexity is that the platform is presented as a self-evident totality, avoiding the complexity of its conditions. (p. 130)*

--> the platform hides its own machinery. It presents itself as simple, as natural, as the only way. The smoothness is a lie. Underneath, there is extraction, complexity, violence. But we only see the surface -- the smooth scroll, the clean interface, the seamless experience. Smoothness as ideology.


*"The force of techno-capitalist progress is inevitable, an inexorable physical process." (p. 131)*

--> this is the most dangerous myth of all. That this is natural. That this is physics. That we can't stop it. It's not physics. It's a choice made by people with money. The inevitability is manufactured.


*"Life persists to consume," as Moby White indicates. It is entropy, the relentless criticism of growth and slowdown. (p. 132)*

--> consumption as the only persistence left. We don't persist to create, to love, to think. We persist to consume. That's what the system needs from us -- not our minds, not our art, not our love. Just our consumption.


*"Allow yourself to go down rabbit holes. A rabbit hole is not a distraction. A rabbit hole is your brain telling you to pay attention to what feels resonant. Pay attention to what feels resonant to you. Save breaking things down and build a path for yourself. Creativity is just a muscle worth exercising. Over time, build contexts for yourself and your ideas. Analyze what happens when you connect those contexts to someone else's. Remember: the goal is not self-improvement. The goal is to go more deeply with the world." (p. 133)*

--> this is beautiful and I want to believe it. But the platform has hijacked the rabbit hole. The rabbit hole used to lead somewhere unexpected. Now the algorithm builds the hole for you, and it leads exactly where your data profile predicts you'll go. The real rabbit hole is the one the algorithm can't predict. The one that takes you somewhere you didn't know you wanted to go.


*"Perfection is the highest stage of depression." (p. 144)*

--> yes. This connects to everything. Perfect production for perfect consumption of perfect content ([[Art in Exocapitalist systems]]). Perfection is the goal of the system, and the system's goal is our depression. Because depressed people consume. Depressed people don't resist. Depressed people scroll.


*The digitalization of music has not opened up any new vistas but instead contributed to the fact that most music is infinitely uniform and boring. (p. 142)*

--> this is what happened to Perseverance. Every time I found meaning in a song, the novelty disappeared. The tools make it too easy to produce and too hard to create. Infinite access, zero depth.


*In his 2024 e-flux essay Internet as Novel, Boris Groys discusses Mikhail Bakhtin's ideal of polyphonic forms. Groys complains that these days, "literature is mostly seen as a platform that allows a writer to give a voice to a social group that previously did not have this voice." This is the ego-centric cult of identity. He contrasts the ego-obsessed with the great novel writer who withdrew their own voices to be able to present the human comedy, in which every social group had its part. (p. 150-151)*

--> the internet promised polyphony but delivered monologue. Everyone speaks, no one listens. Groys is right: the great writers withdrew themselves to let the world speak through them. The platform does the opposite -- it amplifies the self until the world disappears. We are all performing ourselves instead of witnessing each other.


*"As a reaction to the 'voice' of the other -- it says 'I like it,' 'I do not like it,' and then the communication ends. After such a reaction, there is nothing more to say. The voice of the other dies even before this other dies." (p. 152)*

--> like and dislike killed dialogue. Binary reaction replaced conversation. The other person's voice doesn't even get to live long enough to be heard -- it's immediately sorted into like or dislike, and then it's over. The voice dies in the sorting.


*The Australian author of Culture is not an Industry, Justin O'Connor, calls to move out of advocacy into politics. "To do so will not be about a demand for more funding but about reinventing the social as part of the foundations of our common life. One thing we know: just as you don't bring a knife to a gunfight, you don't bring a spreadsheet to a culture war. We need a new language, the only available element to shape a new epoch." (p. 147)*

--> we need a new language. Not a new platform, not a new policy, not a new metric. A new language. Because the current language is the system's language, and you can't dismantle the master's house with the master's tools.


*"If art and culture are for anything, they are to help reimagine a future, a habitable future, and they must do that alongside those who have most to lose if we fall." (p. 148)*


*A mix of singularity and mediocrity, blandness and shyness, rendered by artificial correctness into colour-corrected images. (p. 148)*

--> this is what the platform produces. Not art, not garbage, but something in between: the colour-corrected, AI-smoothed, algorithmically optimized middle. Neither brilliant nor terrible. Just content. Just feed.


*Techno-solutionism and organised optimism kill any attempt to slow down, focus, reflect and question. The what-ever urge to move on prevents any form of radical criticism and even do-good dialogue. (p. 153)*

--> organised optimism as a weapon. "Things are getting better!" "Technology will solve it!" "Just build!" This optimism isn't hope -- it's suppression. It's the system telling you to stop asking questions and keep scrolling.

## My claim

"Perfection is the highest stage of depression." This is the sentence that ties the whole book to everything I've been writing. The perfect production of perfect content for perfect consumption ([[Art in Exocapitalist systems|"what is a framework that is utterly perfect in its production, consumption, and content? A perfect replication of reality; a virtual reality"]]) is the architecture of depression. Not a side effect -- the architecture. The system needs you depressed because depressed people don't create, they consume. They don't resist, they cope ([[Copium Compendium - How do you cope in this digital age|"coping results from losing the positive side of ideology as a belief system"]]). Perfection is the goal, and reaching it means reaching the end -- the end of striving, of hunting, of asking why. The dying animal ([[Voices and the Dying Animal|"the animal is dying bc we're feeding it answers instead of letting it hunt"]]) has been fed the perfect answer, and it stopped breathing.

Groys's complaint about the ego-centric cult of identity is the literary version of what the platform does to all of us. The great novel writer withdrew themselves to let the world through. The platform amplifies the self until the world can't get in. "I like it" / "I do not like it" -- and then the voice of the other dies. This is the opposite of polyphony. This is monophony at scale, millions of isolated voices all performing themselves into a void that sorts them into like and dislike. The feed is not a conversation. It's a cemetery of reactions.

"You don't bring a spreadsheet to a culture war. We need a new language." O'Connor is right, and this is why The Zero matters ([[Book Plan - The Zero|"the only available element to shape a new epoch"]]). The current language is the system's language -- data, metrics, engagement, content, platform, scale. You can't think outside the system in the system's own words. A new language means new concepts, new metaphors, new myths. The loop, the edge, the zero, the queer intelligence that exists between nodes -- this is the language that the system cannot metabolize, because it operates in the incalculable ([[Peak data|"the conflict is one between the calculable and the incalculable"]]).

"The digitalization of music has not opened up any new vistas but instead contributed to the fact that most music is infinitely uniform and boring." This is Perseverance. This is every EP that started with meaning and ended with metrics. The tools are perfect now -- and perfection, remember, is the highest stage of depression. When you can produce anything, you produce nothing. When everything sounds possible, nothing sounds necessary. Creation needs constraint, needs the gap, needs the imperfect ([[Loneliness in the age of social media|"imperfect memory was generative, it made us reach"]]). The democratization of tools was supposed to liberate art. Instead it produced content. Infinite access, zero depth. "A mix of singularity and mediocrity, blandness and shyness, rendered by artificial correctness into colour-corrected images."

"The goal is not self-improvement. The goal is to go more deeply with the world." -- but the platform has hijacked the rabbit hole. It builds personalized tunnels that lead exactly where your data profile predicts. The real rabbit hole is the one the algorithm can't predict. That's where the hunt lives. That's where the animal might still be breathing.
