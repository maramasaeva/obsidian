## My claim

Smartphone mythologies are the folk religion of the telebody ([[Telebodies|"the telebody is neither fully organic nor completely digital, but rather a natural-technical entity"]]). When people say Instagram is listening through the microphone, they're not making a technical claim -- they're making an existential one. They're saying: this thing knows me better than I know myself, and that feels like surveillance. The algorithm's predictive accuracy has exceeded the threshold where it can be distinguished from telepathy. And so we reach for the only framework available: the myth.

Barthes decoded the mythologies of postwar consumer France -- toys, steak, striptease. The objects that shaped everyday life without anyone noticing they were shaped. The smartphone is our steak and chips. But there's a difference: Barthes could separate himself from the objects he decoded. We cannot separate ourselves from the smartphone. It is under our pillow. It is the first thing we touch in the morning and the last thing we touch at night. You can't mythologize what you can't get distance from. This is the same problem as [[Platform futility|"we can't study something from inside it"]]. The fish can't describe water.

"We've gone from why to what and just because." This is the death of narrative. The timeline replaced the story. But a timeline has no arc -- it's pure sequence, one thing after another, with no causality and no resolution. And we've internalized this. We no longer ask why things happen. We just scroll to the next thing. The dying animal ([[Voices and the Dying Animal|"curiosity: the dying animal"]]) hunted by asking why. Now it is fed what. The feed killed the question.

The folk myths -- phone radiation, 5G, the microphone listening -- are not stupidity. They are the only tools people have to name what the platform has made unnameable. The anxiety is real: something is extracting from us, something is changing us, something has power over us. The technical explanation (targeted advertising, predictive algorithms, behavioral data) is accurate but emotionally empty. The myth is inaccurate but emotionally true. People know they are being colonized ([[Debating techno-feudalism|"paying rent for cognitive and social environments"]]). They just don't have the language for it yet. So they invent myths. And myths, at least, contain the question why.
