## My claim

Friendship cannot be optimized. The moment you make friendship the goal -- swipe right for a friend, match on interests, schedule a hangout -- you've entered the logic of production, and friendship is not a product. Friendship is a byproduct of shared experience, of time wasted together, of the unplanned encounter. It is what happens in the edges between nodes, not in the nodes themselves ([[Book Plan - The Zero|"existence in the edges between nodes, being-without-centre"]]). An app that connects you to a friend is connecting two nodes directly. But friendship lives in the edge. The edge can't be manufactured; it emerges.

This is why "making missing sexy again" matters. Constant availability kills the productive absence that friendship needs. You can't miss someone whose stories you scroll past every day. And missing is what gives presence its weight. The telebody ([[Telebodies|"telelife is the condition in which digital relationships take precedence over analogue ones, in which distance is full and proximity empty"]]) inverts this: distance becomes full (of content, of updates, of performance) and proximity becomes empty. We know everything about each other and feel nothing. The feed replaces the ache, and without the ache there's no desire to be close.

"No one misses anyone anymore. The out there remembers for us." -- we've outsourced memory to the platform. And memory is what makes longing possible. If I can look up your profile any time, I never have to hold you in my mind. I never have to reconstruct you from fragments, fill in the gaps with imagination, feel the shape of your absence. The platform remembers perfectly, and perfect memory destroys longing. Imperfect memory -- the kind we used to have -- was generative. It made us reach. Now we scroll.

The "something else" I'm looking for: connection that is neither efficient nor performative. Not the connection of being in the same online community, not the connection of liking each other's posts. Something with opacity in it ([[Voices and the Dying Animal|Xun: "every process of understanding implies a dimension of opacity"]]). The mystery between us is where the work happens. Friendship needs mystery. It needs the gap. It needs the not-knowing. The platform closes every gap, and in doing so, closes the space where friendship could have lived.
