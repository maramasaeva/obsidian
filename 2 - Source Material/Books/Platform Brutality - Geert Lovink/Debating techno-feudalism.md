## My claim

Capitalism still functions, but not for us. It functions for something else. This is the thought that connects Lovink to everything I've been writing about UCI. The "death of capitalism's spirit" is not the death of capitalism -- it's the moment capitalism stopped needing human belief to sustain itself. We lost faith in it, and it didn't care. It kept going. Because it now serves an entity that doesn't require our faith, only our labor and data. This is what Preciado means when describing [[Forced digitalization|the cyberpop gulag]]: "neoliberal societies hoped not only to defeat the virus, but, more importantly, to defeat the unstoppable process of depatriarchalization and decolonization." The system preserves itself through us, not for us.

"Paying rent for cognitive and social environments" -- Varoufakis's cloud feudalism -- is colonialism by other means. The colonial logic is identical: occupy a territory (our attention, our social life, our memory), extract rent, make the occupied population dependent on the occupier's infrastructure. Europeans waking up to their dependency on US cloud services in 2025 is the geopolitical version of what every individual user already knows: I don't want to be here, but there's nothing else ([[Loneliness in the age of social media|"you feel inclined to go, you feel like you have to, you feel like there's nothing else"]]). Voluntary free slavery.

"We're regressing into something, but I don't know what exactly." -- I think I know what it is now. It's feudalism with screens. The regression is from citizen to serf, from participant to spectator, from producer to tenant. And the democracy that remains is the name without the practice ([[Copium Compendium - How do you cope in this digital age|"we cannot do the activities that people should be able to do in a democracy"]]).

"Everything that comes to us online comes to us from the past." This is the slow cancellation of the future made literal. The feed is trained on historical data. The algorithm shows you what people like you have already liked. AI generates from what has already been written. We live in the past tense online. The only thing that can break this is genuine novelty -- something the system has never seen. Something created, not produced. Something that comes from the future, not the archive. This is why [[Art in Exocapitalist systems|"the artist is not the creator, but the medium of creation that art itself uses to Actualise"]] -- art comes from somewhere the algorithm can't reach. That's its power. That's why it matters.
