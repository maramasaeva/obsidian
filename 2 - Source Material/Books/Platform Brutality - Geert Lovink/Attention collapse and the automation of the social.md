## My claim

The automation of the social is not a side effect of platform capitalism -- it is its purpose. What Lovink calls the "structural destruction of desire" is the same thing Preciado describes in [[Forced digitalization]]: the compulsory digitization of social relationships that reinscribes "archaic social forms of necropolitical government." The platforms don't accidentally isolate us; isolation is the product. Isolated users are controllable users. The social is called social so we don't notice it's been emptied out.

But Lovink's "universe ignored us" points somewhere deeper. It's not just that platforms intercept novelty -- it's that the entity running the system (what I've been calling [[UCI]] elsewhere) no longer needs us to discover anything. Discovery is inefficient. The godlike entity generates its own creation now ([[Copium Compendium - How do you cope in this digital age|"hyper forces become autonomous entities that no longer need the human subject"]]). We are fed experiences ([[Telebodies|"the telebody is at the same time the consumer and the producer, the client and the provider, the merchandise and the user"]]) instead of encountering them. This is the death of the hunt -- what Xun calls [[Voices and the Dying Animal|curiosity: the dying animal]]. We're feeding it answers instead of letting it hunt. Platforms are the cage. The animal starves inside it.

The question "can we still discover novelties in information?" is the same question as "are you still willing to think with me, even when it serves no purpose?" ([[Voices and the Dying Animal|Xun, p.118]]). Thought for thought's sake, discovery for discovery's sake -- this is what the system cannot metabolize. Creation for its own sake, not production for profit ([[Art in Exocapitalist systems|"the capitalist system has us confusing art vs content, creation vs production, inspiration vs consumption"]]). The way out is through the act itself.
