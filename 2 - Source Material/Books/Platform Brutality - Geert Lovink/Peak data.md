## My claim

Data is the new plastic. Both are byproducts of overproduction that the system cannot metabolize. Both accumulate. Both poison the environment they fill. And both were sold to us as progress -- plastic as the miracle material, data as the miracle knowledge. The promise was the same: more is better, everything can be captured, nothing needs to be lost. But "nothing needs to be lost" is the same logic that killed missing ([[Loneliness in the age of social media|"no one misses anyone anymore. The out there remembers for us"]]). The platform remembers perfectly, and perfect memory is perfect data is perfect pollution.

"The conflict is one between the calculable and the incalculable." This is the same conflict as creation vs. production ([[Art in Exocapitalist systems|"the capitalist system has us confusing art vs content, creation vs production"]]). Production is calculable -- metrics, KPIs, engagement rates, impact factors. Creation is incalculable -- you cannot measure what a poem does to someone. The system, which can only process the calculable, therefore declares that the incalculable doesn't exist. Or more precisely: it converts the incalculable into the calculable. A song becomes a stream count. A friendship becomes a follower count. A thought becomes a like. And in the conversion, the thing that mattered -- the incalculable thing -- dies.

"Data are truth, muddy and silent by decay." The data prophet looks backward and calls it vision. This is the same temporal trap as [[Debating techno-feudalism|"everything that comes to us online comes to us from the past"]]. The algorithm is trained on historical data. The metrics measure what already happened. Data is always the corpse of experience, never the experience itself. And yet we worship it. Dataism is the religion of the dead ([[Copium Compendium - How do you cope in this digital age|"with history and technology further speeding up, the hyper forces become autonomous entities that no longer need the human subject"]]).

"Wouldn't it be a relief to eliminate the data ways?" -- this is the most honest question in the book so far. Not "how do we fix data" or "how do we make data ethical" but: what if we just stopped? What if we refused the promontory entirely? The dying animal ([[Voices and the Dying Animal|"curiosity: the dying animal"]]) doesn't need data to hunt. It needs the gap, the unknown, the darkness ahead. Data fills the darkness with light, and in doing so, kills the hunt.
