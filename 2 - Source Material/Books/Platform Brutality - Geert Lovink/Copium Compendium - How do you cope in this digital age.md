## My claim

Coping killed my hobbies. The psych ward instrumentalized everything I loved -- reading, drawing, painting, running, making music -- by turning them into therapeutic tools. The moment an activity becomes a means to an end (even the end of "getting better"), it stops being the thing it was. Music-as-coping is not music. It's medicine that sounds like music. And medicine you didn't choose tastes like obligation. So eventually I stopped doing all of it and scrolled instead, because scrolling doesn't pretend to mean anything.

This is the same mechanism that hollowed out Perseverance. Every time I found meaning in a song, the novelty disappeared, and with it the meaning. Because meaning doesn't survive being instrumentalized. It exists only in the act, in the hunt ([[Voices and the Dying Animal|"the animal is dying bc we're feeding it answers instead of letting it hunt"]]). When creation becomes production -- when I make a song because I should, because it's good for me, because it's my coping strategy -- it enters the logic of [[Art in Exocapitalist systems|perfect production for perfect consumption]]. And that logic is a cage.

Toska is what's left when even irony fails. Irony contains a gap between what is and what should be -- and that gap is a form of hope. Toska has collapsed that gap. There is nothing left to be ironic about. This is the post-ironic condition of the platform user: not angry, not sad, just whatever. Copium. The "static state of whatever."

But: "reclaim the social. It is now or never." And what I wrote -- experience novelty head on instead of scrolling for it -- that's the answer the book doesn't give concretely. The practice creates the practitioner ([[Voices and the Dying Animal|Xun, p.73: "Prompt Thinking generates agency through the practice itself"]]). You don't need to be ready first. You don't need to have reclaimed yourself before you start. The act of connecting IS the reclamation. The deja vu breaks the moment something genuinely surprises you -- and surprise requires risk, which requires leaving the feed.
