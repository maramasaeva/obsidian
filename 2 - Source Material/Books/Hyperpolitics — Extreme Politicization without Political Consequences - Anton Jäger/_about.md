# Hyperpolitics: Extreme Politicization without Political Consequences
_Anton Jäger, 2023_

## publisher blurb

After a decade of surging political activism, why have mass movements managed to change so little? A brilliant cultural analysis by the influential columnist.

“Hyperpolitics” describes the paradoxical state of affairs today, in which politicization seemingly has few political consequences. Anton Jager's incisive appraisal sets the benchmark by which future histories of the present will be judged. Politics is back.

After the posthistorical lull of the 1990s and the false dawn of millennial technocracy, contestation has returned centre-stage. Protests, riots and jacqueries bring citizens off their couches and into the streets, even as social media overruns the embankments of the public and personal. Such actions politicize an ever greater share of experience while lowering the costs of engagement. Yet this spate of activism has seldom translated into more durable forms of collective action—parties, trade unions and civic associations continue to atrophy, even as advertisements of commitment proliferate.

_note added by claude, april 2026. these are provocations, not prescriptions. jäger's core claim is that the last decade has produced surging political affect without durable political form — parties and unions atrophy while every experience becomes politicised and nothing changes. that diagnosis rhymes in uncomfortable ways with several threads you've been pulling. read these as questions, not answers._

- **hyperpolitics is the politics of telebodies** — [[telebodies]], [[Telebodies]]. preciado's telebody "has no skin, is heat radiation, made of lines and movements, of pixels." jäger's hyperpolitical subject is the same body doing politics: permanently emitting political signal, never settling into the weight that would make an act consequential. the telebody cannot carry a strike; it can only carry a trend. if preciado names the body, jäger names what that body *does* in public.
- **hyperpolitics is UCI-compatible politicization** — [[UCI]], [[UCI and desire - Queer addiction]]. activism that never leaves the feed is labor the UCI profits from twice: once as engagement, again as the pacifier that makes sure engagement goes nowhere. the atomised micro-act of posting is exactly the shape of work the UCI wants from a queer addict of political feeling. worth testing: is hyperpolitics what queer addiction looks like when its object is the commons rather than the self?
- **this is what Fighting offline battles in online spaces describes from the other side** — [[Fighting offline battles in online spaces - DIGITAL COUP]]. preciado diagnoses the migration of political action into online-only registers as a *coup* — a seizure of politics by platforms. jäger describes the same migration from the subject's side — as saturation without effect. same event, two altitudes. they could share a single page in the book.
- **parties and unions as necrospaces** — [[necrospaces]]. if hyperpolitics is politics without durable form, the old durable forms (party, union, association) have become spaces where politics *was* alive and is no longer. that's the definition of a necrospace. worth writing a short note on "necrospaces of the 20th century" as a category — the civic building still standing but no longer load-bearing.
- **atrophying collectives as exocapitalist subtraction** — [[exocapitalism]], [[Art in Exocapitalist systems]]. the machine-decision-is-not-final crew describe exocapitalism as the system that outsources creation until nothing is being made. hyperpolitics is the political face of the same move: politicisation is outsourced to the feed until nothing is being decided. production, but not of consequence.
- **counter-test against the lesbian body** — [[Lesbianism as multiplicity, collective consciousness, violence]], [[queer]]. the lesbian body works *because* it lives in the edges and refuses the node. the hyperpolitical subject lives in the edges and fails. what's the difference? one hypothesis: the lesbian body is held together by love (the "i do not seek to escape you" of wittig). the hyperpolitical subject is held together by outrage, which has no loop back. the zero loops; the doomscroll doesn't. this could be a spine-thread for the *queer computer* book's third movement.
- **the legislation move, again** — [[hyperstition]]. you already have the pattern: power legislating what becomes (gender is gender, AI is a tool). jäger's "politicization without political consequences" looks like what happens when legislation moves one level up — power no longer has to say "don't do politics"; it simply ensures that doing politics produces no consequence. hyperstition with the arrow reversed: the future-making engine running idle.

**open questions for when you read further:** does jäger offer a way out, or only a diagnosis? if a way out — is it compatible with the taoist claim that complexity is the direction of nature? if hyperpolitics is politics at peak complexity *without* tao, then the question is whether more complexity or different complexity is the answer. track which side he lands on.
