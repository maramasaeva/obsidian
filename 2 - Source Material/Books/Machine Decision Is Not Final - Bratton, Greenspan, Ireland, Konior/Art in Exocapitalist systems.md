My claim:
The capitalist system has us confusing certain terms that are related, but certainly mean different things: art vs content, creation vs production, inspiration vs consumption. Human artefacts have become centred on marginalization; the production of some margin in order to use that profit to produce something else. Production for the sake of other production only, production in order to produce more other things, and not for the act of production itself. 

This production of content must be perfect, and so must the way we consume content. The producers and consumers themselves, as well, must be the perfect content. Because the system is forcing us, on all three sides of the equation (producer, consumer, content) to be error-free, to produce only what is perfect, and to be only perfect, it has become immensely troublesome, a great struggle, to do something else.

That other thing, production purely for the act of it, is creation. Consuming only for the act of it, is inpiration. The thing itself is not content, then (produced to be consumed) but art (created to be inspired by; active, not passive!). When we create (not produce) art, we're then not really creating something other than itself; rather, we're creating something that already existed, that was already Real, but had yet to take form in the Actual. The artist, in continuous feedback [[loops]], creates not art, but Actualizes it as it takes shape. The artist is not the creator, but the medium of creation that art itself uses to Actualise. In other words, art follows the principle of [[spyrodynamism]], and does this by utilizing the human artist, in our case. It might utilise other media in other dimensions, or levels of the spiral or other nodes of the network ([[cybernetics]])

When we allow the train of thought of the perfect production and perfect consumption of perfect content, what framework do those elements live inside? What is a framework, a system or map, that is utterly perfect in its production, consumption, and content? A perfect replication of reality; a virtual reality. The perfect production of perfect content, for the perfect consumption of perfect consumers, can only exist in a reality that is virtual in its essence, as well as artificial. It has to be produced itself, and this as well perfectly.
