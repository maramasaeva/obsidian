*Either way, you will see before you the strands of Fate. The World-Lines branch out in front of you, maybe two, maybe two thousand, maybe a number so high it is incomprehensible with your current hardware.*

*Either way, you will have to choose. Every choice you make is a spell cast. Make the right choice or make the wrong choice - just be sure to choose.*

*>>>>>Every choice made is a spell cast.<<<<<*

*Yes, even the little choices. Especially the little choices. But this is not a reason to take things too seriously. Do you like reading novels where the main character is neurotic about making the right decision all the time? Of course not. Don't be that sort of character. Trust yourself. But make the choice.*

Source's claim: every decision — especially the small ones — is a magickal act. decisions are not preceded by magick; they *are* magick. the correlate: paralysis by deliberation is worse than "wrong" choice, because indecision is itself the casting of a spell of stasis.

My claim: _seed — claude, april 2026. the provocation here is the dissolution of the distinction between "deciding what to do" and "doing magick." both are world-line selection. this cuts against a certain kind of over-deliberated rationalist stance (which mc aodha names a few pages later). compatible with your [[Time-sorcery is a Marxist Taoistic Queer Computer practice]] thread: if present behaviour shapes which future is summoned, every present choice is already a spell. what this adds: **the spell cast badly is more powerful than the spell not cast at all.**_
