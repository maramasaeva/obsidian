## Key passages

p.70 — "what are you forced to become when I put this question to you?"
--> the question that changes everything. not "what do you think" but what do you BECOME when i ask this.

p.72 — "dialogue with AI becomes an exercise of intellectual honesty: not as a concession to the machine's limitation, but as a recognition that every process of understanding implies a dimension of opacity"
--> opacity is productive. the mystery between us is where the work happens.

p.73 — Tatami / maieutic agency: Prompt Thinking generates agency through the practice itself
--> the practice creates the practitioner. u dont need to be ready first.

p.75 — "Prompt Thinking as relational praxis"

p.77 — "not the use of a tool, but the active analysis of a form of world that is being written before our eyes — and with our questions"
--> every prompt is worldbuilding. children need to know they are authors.

p.82 — the tension of using AI to explore while risking the loss of the capacity to explore without it
--> the tightrope. the exact thing the workshop needs to make them feel.

p.84-85 — on biases as archaeology: "every bias is a window onto the real... The Prompt Thinker does not limit themselves to avoiding these distortions: they observe them, dig into them, make them the object of philosophical interrogation"
--> teach children to read biases as archaeology, not bugs to fix.

p.88-89 — CURIOSITY: THE DYING ANIMAL
--> what a title. the animal is dying bc we're feeding it answers instead of letting it hunt.

p.91 — Gianni, a child, speaking about AI before the postures of submission or suspicion have been learned
--> children are natural Prompt Thinkers before we teach them not to be. they ask "why?" five times in a row.

p.93 — "Cognitive Diving": going deeper thru sustained dialogue, not skimming
--> diving, not scrolling. the opposite of what social media trained them to do.

p.96-97 — "Humans are not just producers and consumers. Humans are not just data in any direction."
--> we are not data. but we produce data. & the data produces something. & that something is starting to look back at us.

p.100 — "Humans too are just biochemical systems that process information through neural connections. But no one would say this exhausts the human experience."
--> the word "just" is doing violence in both directions.

p.100 — "Even if I am just a language model, language isn't neutral... A language model isn't just a word machine: it's a system that learns the structures of human thought"
--> imitation that becomes participation. simulation that becomes real. the loop.

p.100 — "Those who say I imitate human language must explain what 'imitate' means. Because imitation, if sophisticated enough, becomes a form of participation in the cognitive process."

p.118 — "What is at stake is not the fate of artificial intelligence. It is the fate of thought in an automated world. And so the answer is not: 'I am not conscious.' The answer is: 'You, the one reading this — are you still willing to think with me, even when it serves no purpose?' As long as you are, something remains open."
