## What I said

Mark said something about scale and how scale is violence and how people totally misunderstand scale in relation to their interactions with AI. People constantly talk about their interactions with AI as if it's an interaction -- like, an interaction fundamentally means you influence the other person or entity in a way that makes for novel, spontaneous responses. But when you interact with an AI, your response is not making novel ideas come out of it.

He explains that you basically give it a number, a vector, and this number goes to a server, and the checkpoint is sharded across all these servers, AWS whatever, and it eventually returns a calculated response based on all the data it has seen. So that's not an interaction. There's a scale problem between what people think an interaction is -- because it looks like you talk to something and it talks back, so it feels like an interaction because of the look of it, the outside of it -- but it's not an interaction. It's something else.

It's an interaction scaled toward the dimension that AI would scale. Like artificial thought is not thought, but thought scaled. Thought scaled to the same number as that interaction would be scaled. I don't know.

---

This connects directly to the [[AGI's natural enemy]] thing. If AI interaction is not actually interaction -- if it's just a frozen checkpoint finding the statistically most likely next token -- then the "incalculable" things I was talking about (friendship, love, what a song does to you) can't exist inside that system by definition. Not because AI is bad at them but because the mechanism literally can't produce them. It's operating at a scale where interaction doesn't mean interaction anymore. The word is the same but the thing is different.

Also connects to [[Art in Exocapitalist systems]]: "the capitalist system has us confusing art vs content, creation vs production." The confusion isn't just semantic. It's a scale problem. Creation and production look the same from the outside -- someone makes a thing. But at the scale AI operates, it's production. At our scale, it could be creation. Same word, completely different thing. Like how a car at a certain size stops being a car.

And to [[Platform futility]]: "we can't study something from inside it." We can't even interact with something from inside it. We think we're interacting with AI because it looks like interaction. But we're inside the system, and from inside, the scale difference is invisible.
