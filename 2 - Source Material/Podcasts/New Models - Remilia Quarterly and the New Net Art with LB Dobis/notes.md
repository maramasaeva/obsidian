## Key concepts from the podcast

**Remilia as decentralized art collective**: Started ~2021 from group chats organized by Charlotte Fang. A convergence of Twitter posters, 4chan writers, crypto finance, and NEETs. Not a traditional art collective — a corporate entity that plays with the internet. Funded through NFTs (Milady) which bootstrapped the construction of what they call "the new internet."

**Posting as art form**: Milady posting operates through directed formulas — set a template (cute anime girl + violence, schizo-posting, Chinese posting formula), combine with stream-of-consciousness "tip of your mind" writing, and scale through thousands of people doing it at once. The content almost doesn't matter. What matters is operating at the protocol level — creating a wave you can flow along to reach others, overriding algorithmic defaults.

**Hacking vs critique**: The millennial mode was critique — self-aware art that performed its knowledge of its own commodification. A parlor game that wasn't actually doing anything. Remilia's mode is hacking — working *within* the system to maximum effect rather than subverting or resisting it. The hacker mode is more effective than the critical mode.

**The internet as flat reality**: Millennials maintained ironic distance from the internet — "I'm representing reality online, and there's a dissonance between what I represent and the true reality." Remilia takes the internet as given. No drive to bring it offline. No dissonance. Online IS reality. The people they attract are NEETs who are online 24/7 engaging with it as reality. This is a fundamental framing shift.

**Art degenerating into advertising**: By the 2010s, individual artists online had degenerated into advertisements. Genuine artists couldn't engage with the new modes or refused to debase themselves. Institutions were captured by a performance of woke politics. The most interesting artists by the 2020s didn't call themselves artists and were all ending up online.

**Viral Public License (VPL)**: Milady operates under a non-license — images can be reproduced, destroyed, used in marketing. The only requirement is derivatives must also be under VPL. Stark contrast to Bored Ape model ("own your ape, make a movie"). Value isn't in the content or the labor put into it. Value is in the cultural capital — the NFT is a tribal signifier that captures value from the subcultures being platformed.

**Networks as the new art worlds**: The traditional art world dissolved in the 2010s. Remilia hasn't just made a new network — it's made its own art world. You don't need validation from another art world because that art world doesn't really exist anymore. Maybe networks are the new art worlds. Each network with its own protocol of value, communication, social status.

**No common public sphere**: Critical gestures only worked when linear media created a common public sphere — everyone saw it. With decentralization of media came decentralization of attention. Remilia assumes there is no common public sphere. There is only the timeline. Your timeline doesn't look like mine. The goal is velocity — making your post stay at the top.
