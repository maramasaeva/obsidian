Citarella brings up George Lakoff's framework: conservative politics is "daddy politics" (strict father, discipline, punishment, loyalty) and liberal politics is "mommy politics" (nurturing, care, community, empathy). The right wants a strong daddy. The left wants a good mommy. And people's feelings about politicians are deep transferences of feelings about parents.

ContraPoints takes this further. She's gotten into psychoanalysis recently -- specifically Freud, not Jung ("a little bit of a crystal girl for my taste"). Her argument: the appeal of Trump, the appeal of fascism -- there's some libidinal thing here. Something unconscious. Calling it madness is a non-explanation. Psychoanalyzing the political unconscious is where she's headed in her work.

The rational-irrational split connects too. New Atheism was an Enlightenment movement -- a fetish for reason and logic. But the people who claimed to be supremely rational were demonstrably not so. This pushed ContraPoints toward understanding the emotional, irrational dimensions of politics that Marxist materialism tends to neglect.

The daddy/mommy politics framework connects to [[Your gender as public technology]]: "I didn't choose my gender, because gender is not something we choose, but something we collectively make: a political technology." If gender is a collective political technology, then so is the daddy/mommy dynamic in politics. We don't choose to want a strong father figure -- it's collectively made, socially constructed, and then instrumentalized by the right.

The looksmaxing point from earlier in the podcast also fits here -- Mara's video on [[Right-wing aesthetics vs left-wing reality]] already touches this: "even those who used to be in power, men, have now succumbed to the same things that women have for years had to abide by." The capitalist gaze puts everyone under the male gaze. Men are now performing for daddy too. The looksmaxing phenomenon is men discovering they're also being looked at, also being evaluated, also reduced to their appearance. This is what the [[Telebodies|telebody]] does: "the consumer and the producer, the client and the provider, the merchandise and the user." Everyone is merchandise now.
