Citarella makes a point that I think is one of the most important in the whole conversation: the left has internalized the libertarian values of the people who built social media. Everything must be visible. Everything must be transparent. Every position must be stated publicly.

But historically, political movements had offstage spaces. Closed-door meetings. Internal conversations. You could work through difficult ideas without performing them for the public. Inside the party, you knew where you stood. Outside, you presented what would actually win people over. This isn't dishonesty -- it's political strategy that every successful movement in history has used.

ContraPoints agrees and says it better: "There's no offstage now. Everything's onstage. And as a result, nuances can't be worked through. You almost can't think, because you're always performing. And thinking means considering wrong ideas, experimenting with things that go too far."

Social media cannot be the only forum for political conversation. It's too public. It attracts audiences it was never meant for. Screenshots get shared out of context. People who would have been kicked out of a meeting for secretly recording are now the default condition of every online interaction.

---

This is a massive insight. The platform doesn't just prevent organizing (as Lovink says in [[Attention collapse and the automation of the social|"the obstacles to identifying with others, coming together and taking action are growing"]]). It prevents *thinking*. You can't think in public. Thinking requires trying wrong ideas, going too far, being uncertain. All of that gets punished online. So the left doesn't think -- it performs. It performs purity. And purity is the enemy of strategy.

This connects to Jäger's [[Hyperpolitics]] again: "politics without infrastructure." The infrastructure isn't just organizational -- it's spatial. You need a room that isn't being recorded. You need a conversation that doesn't have an audience. The platform architecture makes this impossible. Discord servers are the closest thing, but even those leak.

Also connects to what Lovink says about the [[Copium Compendium - How do you cope in this digital age|"static state of whatever"]]. If you can't think, you can't strategize. If you can't strategize, you cope. The left is coping because it has no space to think. Not because it lacks ideas, but because it lacks *rooms*.

And to [[Debating techno-feudalism]]: "is social media use a form of voluntary free slavery?" -- yes, and part of what's enslaved is the ability to have a private political thought.
