ContraPoints opens the episode with this: "The West has fallen, why? Because of degeneracy. And the evidence is like -- look at modern architecture compared to a cathedral." Right-wing arguments about civilizational decline are almost always aesthetic arguments. Not policy. Not economics. Vibes. Look at this pretty thing we lost. The image attached to "we used to be a proper country" is a 7-Eleven ad from the 1970s.

This is Walter Benjamin's old point about fascism: it aestheticizes politics. It turns political questions into questions of beauty, style, order. The cathedral vs the brutalist building. The classical statue vs the abstract sculpture. The tradwife vs the blue-haired feminist. It's all visual. It's all vibes.

---

This is exactly what I was talking about in [[Right-wing aesthetics vs left-wing reality]]. Left-wing arguments are based on reality -- healthcare, equality, fair treatment of actual people. Right-wing arguments are based on aesthetics -- look at what we're losing, look at how ugly things have gotten.

But the contradiction: the reality people make abstract art. The aesthetics people make realistic art. The right is hyper-realistic, romantic, detailed. The left is conceptual, experimental, open to interpretation. And in an attention economy where you have a split second to make someone stop scrolling, beauty wins. The right knows this. Remilia, Milady -- they don't need you to read the argument. They just need you to like the image.

The connection to [[Nomos of the network]] is also here: "a mix of singularity and mediocrity, blandness and shyness, rendered by artificial correctness into colour-corrected images." The platform flattens everything into visual content, and in that flattened space, whoever makes the prettiest picture wins. The left keeps trying to win with arguments. Arguments take time. Beauty takes a split second.

And this connects to [[Art in Exocapitalist systems]]: "the capitalist system has us confusing art vs content, creation vs production." The right has figured out how to make content that looks like art. The left is still trying to make art that works as content. Different game entirely.
