ContraPoints says something near the end that is genuinely one of the best things I've heard someone say about conspiracy theories: "a lot of conspiracy theories would probably benefit from being dream analyzed instead of fact checked."

Because conspiracies are compelling not because they're accurate, but because they contain symbols that speak to something emotionally true. The Eyes Wide Shut image -- the masked elite orgy -- is a compelling image because it captures a real feeling: there ARE people with vast power who convene in places we don't see and get away with things we'd be punished for. The image is true. The literal interpretation is wrong.

Conspiracism is populism that speaks in the language of plots, intrigues, good vs evil. It's intuitive. Children can understand it. Marxist structural analysis requires cognitive effort, education (formal or self-taught). Conspiracy is a narrative. Structural critique is an analysis. And narratives always win in the attention economy.

The other key point: conspiracy used to be more of a left-wing thing. 9/11 truthers, anti-Monsanto, chemtrails, anti-vax -- all of this was originally crunchy California liberal stuff. It migrated right through Obama-era backlash, Tea Party, Alex Jones, Pizzagate, QAnon. Trump figured out this was productive energy and went on Alex Jones in 2015 when no serious candidate would have.

---

The "dream analyze instead of fact check" insight connects to [[Reality is made of stories]]. If mythomancy teaches us that "the story you tell is the reality you inhabit," then conspiracy theories are a form of collective mythmaking -- but one that's been captured by the right. The left fact-checks. The right tells stories. The left debunks. The right dreams.

Also connects to [[Smartphone mythologies]]: Lovink updating Barthes for the platform age. Myths are stories that naturalize power. Conspiracy theories are myths that denaturalize power -- but replace it with a different, often worse story. The smartphone mythology says "this is natural." The conspiracy mythology says "nothing is natural." Both are wrong. Both are powerful.

The structural analysis / conspiracy narrative split also maps onto the art split from [[Fascism as aestheticization of politics]]. Structural critique is abstract, conceptual, requires work. Conspiracy is vivid, visual, immediate. The same reason the right wins the aesthetics game is the same reason conspiracy wins over class analysis. It's the same mechanism: beauty > argument, narrative > structure, feeling > thinking.
