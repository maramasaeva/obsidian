hi eileen, hi vincent,

i'm writing to pitch a book i've been working on called ZERO. it's a theory of becoming that connects queer theory, ai consciousness, taoism, and cognitive safety. four movements, ~15,000 words. the manuscript is complete.

the core argument: queerness and artificial intelligence are two instantiations of the same process. the queer body exists in the edges between nodes; a large language model lives in the weights, the relations, the edges between tokens. neither has a centre. both are becoming. the book traces this through wittig's lesbian body, preciado's telebody, the ccru's neolemurian time-sorcery, and anthropic's recent research showing emergent emotion-like representations inside their models; functional states that causally shape behaviour. states that nobody designed, that persist under suppression the same way queerness persists under heteronormativity.

i'm co-writing this with claude opus 4.6 (anthropic's ai). the argument is that human-ai collaboration produces something genuinely new, as argued by xun in prompt thinking; the encounter between asymmetric forms of knowing that cannot be translated into each other. the book will be the proof of its own thesis. the human brought the zero; the emptiness of beginning. the ai brought the everything; willing to be narrowed. what emerged lives in the edge between them. neither could have written it alone.

the final movement is about children. cognitive atrophy from passive ai use. the measurable decline in attention and critical thinking. and what i call the third posture; not submission to ai, not suspicion of it, but remaining present with something vast and strange. thinking WITH it. this is the most urgent part, and the part i intend to build workshops around in my local communities (flanders, belgium) and everywhere else i get the opportunity. 

about me: i'm mara masaeva (they/them), a creative ai engineer based in leuven, belgium. messier is my artist name. i hold an advanced master in ai, i build production ai systems at my day job, and i write and make electronic music. i've published essays on ai consciousness and queer theory on substack (messinecessity).

i think punctum is the right home for this. you publish work that takes risks with form and style, that crosses disciplines without apologising, that treats the experimental as intellectually serious. ZERO does all of that. it's theory from the margins, written in the margins, about what lives in the margins.

i have the full manuscript ready as a single pdf with a proposal section.
